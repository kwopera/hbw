Biography

Chinese mezzo-soprano and Jette Parker Young Artist Hongni Wu makes her Royal Opera debut in the 2018/19 Season as Flora (La traviata) and also sings Mercédès (Carmen).

Wu is a winner of the Metropolitan Opera National Council Auditions 2018. She completed her Professional Studies and Master of Music Degree at Manhattan School of Music where she was the recipient of Stan Sesser Career Award. Wu received her Bachelor of Music Degree from Xinghai Conservatory of Music. At Manhattan School of Music she has sung Cenerentola (La cenerentola), Sesto (La clemenza di Tito), Dragonfly (L'Enfant et les sortilèges). Other appearances include Prince Orlofsky (Die Fledermaus) with Martina Arroyo Foundation, Cherubino (Le nozze di Figaro) with Opera Maine, Carmen with Xinghai Conservatory of Music and Mrs Umeya (Qingling Song) at Guangzhou Opera House. She has participated in the Bel Canto in Tuscany and Scuola di Bel Canto summer programms in Italy.

Wu has participated in concerts and recitals at Guangzhou Opera House, Guangzhou Xinghai Concert Hall, Shanghai Conservatory of Music, Yangzhou University of Music, Nanjing University of Music, and in the CCTV 15th National Young Singers' Concert in Beijing, She has also sung for Martina Arroyo Prelude Gala Concert and Spring Luncheon Cabaret in New York.

Reviews

Hongni Wu, 23, a mezzo-soprano, brought technical agility, warm colorings and an ample sound to “Cruda sorte,” a bel canto showpiece from Rossini’s “L’Italiana in Algeri.” Then, after intermission, in a bold choice, she sang the opening scene of Strauss’s “Der Rosenkavalier” with youthful bloom and richness. We’ll have to see if she follows the path of the coloratura-happy Marilyn Horne or the refined, lyrical Susan Graham.

The New York Times
Anthony Tommasini

Wu’s voice blossomed into a wide array of colors and emotions. She developed into her character and proved to be a remarkably outstanding voice.

OperaWire
Jennifer Pyron

Ms. Wu has a distinctive mezzo-soprano instrument that has unique overtones and resonance. It sounds like chocolate tastes, rich and creamy. But there is a sparkle to it as well. This puts her head and shoulders above the many mezzos who all sound alike. Moreover, she has an abundance of onstage presence and the ability to get a song across.

Voce di Meche
Meche Kroop
