Biography

Chinese mezzo-soprano and Jette Parker Young Artists Hongni Wu makes her Royal Opera debut in the 2018/19 Season as Flora (La traviata) and also sings Mercédès (Carmen).

Wu is a winner of the Metropolitan Opera National Council Auditions 2018. She studied at Xinghai Conservatory of Music, and at the Manhattan School of Music under Joan Patenaude-Yarnell. For Manhattan School of Music she has sung Cenerentola (La cenerentola), Sesto (La clemenza di Tito), Dragonfly (L'Enfant et les sortilèges) and Prince Orlofsky (Die Fledermaus). Other appearances include Cherubino (Le nozze di Figaro) for Opera Maine, Carmen with Xinghai Conservatory of Music and Mrs Umeya (Qingling Song) at Guangzhou Opera House. She has participated in the Bel Canto Summer Programme in Italy.

Wu has participated in concerts and recitals at Shanghai Conservatory of Music, Yangzhou University of Music, Nanjing University of Music, and in the CCTV 15th National Young Singers' Concert in Beijing, a Verdi concert and the Celebration of the 65th Anniversary of the Founding of the PRC in Ghuanzhou. She has also sung in the WQXR Robert Sherman Young Artist Showcase, a Martina Arroyo Prelude Gala Concert in New York and a Martina Arroyo Foundation Spring Luncheon Cabaret.
